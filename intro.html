Lighting Comparison!

  • The 3 types of bulbs considered are Incandescent Tungsten, CFLs and LEDs.
  • We have compared these bulbs based on the 4 critical parameters- Average Life Span, Average Operating Cost, Average Efficiency and Average Purchasing Cost.
  • Selecting a bulb depends upon the type of use, usage time, operating cost etc.
  • Incandescent bulbs are cheap but have a life span of only 1 year and produce less power as compared to CFL or LEDs.
  • CFL bulbs are expensive than Incandescent but have a life span of 8 years and are less costly to maintain.
  • LED bulbs are the most expensive but run for upto 25 years and are most power efficient.
  • A 60W Incandescent produces light equal to a 15W CFL and only a 10W LED.
  • LED bulbs are also low maintainance and only cost around $1.2 per year per bulb as compared to $2.5 for CFL and $11 for an equivalent Incandescent bulb.
