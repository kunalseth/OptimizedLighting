Lighting Comparison!

  • The 3 types of bulbs considered are Incandescent Tungsten, CFLs and LEDs.
  • We have compared these bulbs based on the 4 critical parameters- Average Life Span, Average Operating Cost, Average Efficiency and Average Purchasing Cost.
  • Selecting a bulb depends upon the type of use, usage time, operating cost etc.
  • Incandescent bulbs are cheap but have a life span of only 1 year and produce less power as compared to CFL or LEDs.
  • CFL bulbs are expensive than Incandescent but have a life span of 8 years and are less costly to maintain.
  • LED bulbs are the most expensive but run for upto 25 years and are most power efficient.
  • A 60W Incandescent produces light equal to a 15W CFL and only a 10W LED.
  • LED bulbs are also low maintainance and only cost around $1.2 per year per bulb as compared to $2.5 for CFL and $11 for an equivalent Incandescent bulb.

Measuring Light Output
Lumen Methodology of Lighting Design:

A lumen is a measurement of light, just like watts, which measure the power consumed by the bulb. The brightness of every bulb varies and the amount can be quantified by the lumens value. The higher the lumen rating, the greater the light output. Every room has a different requirement of lighting to perform the tasks at hand. For example, the kitchen needs the highest lumens to be perfect for the tasks.

What's the best for you?

As you have learnt from the bulb camparision in the previous visualization, every bulb has its own features and in here we try to leverage the best propositions that each one brings to the table. For instance, purchasing an incandescent bulb might be cheaper upfront, but the cost of operating is high with poor lifespan causing you to change it faster.

User Preferences

In our solution, we simplify the decision making by giving you 4 options:
  1. Low Power Consumption (KWH)
  2. Low Cost (Cost of purchasing + operating costs in a year)
  3. Eco Friendly (Least emissions)
  4. Custom Setting
Note: The cost is calculated based on the national average $0.12 per KWH of electricity via. US Energy Information Administration as of December 2015.
